Introduction

My name is Abdulahi Adnan Mohammed.

I work in textile design, combining fabric manipulation, texture, and colour development to create fabrics that can be used for garments, accessories, and interior pieces.

I am interested in learning new ways to integrate contemporary methods into my textile practice while still reflecting my own cultural references and everyday stories.

Practice and Focus Areas

In my current practice, I enjoy:

  • Developing fabric samples through pleating, gathering, and other manipulation methods.
  • Experimenting with layered colour on cloth using simple printing and painting techniques.
  • Exploring how texture can change the mood and function of a fabric.
  • Translating hand-drawn ideas into repeatable surface patterns.

My goal is to move from individual experiments to a more organised portfolio of collections that can be used by fashion designers, product makers, and interior decorators.

Skills and Tools

I am steadily building a foundation in textile techniques and tools, including:

  • Basic fabric manipulation (pleats, tucks, gathers, appliqué).
  • Simple printing approaches such as stamping, stencilling, and brushwork on fabric.
  • Colour testing on cotton and blended fabrics.
  • Basic use of sewing machines for finishing edges and joining panels.
  • Organising samples in a clear way for presentation and review.

As I grow, I want to combine these skills with digital embroidery and more advanced surface design so that my textiles can move from single samples into strong, connected collections.

Portfolio Highlights

Below are example directions of work that represent how I like to approach textile projects.

Sample Collection A – Mixed Print Panels
Brightly printed cotton fabrics laid out as panels, testing how different colours, scales and motifs can sit together on one surface for garments, accessories or table pieces.
Sample Collection B – Hand-Printed Rose Motifs
Hand-printed rose motifs created with a block on plain cloth, focusing on spacing, repeat rhythm and even colour across the surface for future yardage or placement prints.
Sample Collection C – Block-Printed Pattern Length
A continuous length of fabric printed with a repeating block, practising alignment, pressure control and building a strong, all-over pattern that can be cut into garments or home pieces.

These sample directions can be expanded into full collections with proper documentation, naming, and technical notes.

Future Direction

Going forward, I want to organise my textile work into small, themed collections and present them in a professional way. I also want to collaborate with fashion designers and product makers who are looking for original fabrics that combine texture, colour, and story.
